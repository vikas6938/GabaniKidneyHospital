Lithotripsy (ESWL)

We have extracorporeal shock wave Lithotripsy unit on which we have successfully performed ESWL of more than 5000 patients of renal & ureteric stone from 0.6 to 2 cm size in this patients. In this procedure, patient does not need hospitalization or any anesthesia. Patient done not need any rest & can resume duty same day. ESWL procedure in properly selected patient gives excellent results.

Lithotripsy is a medical procedure used to break down kidney stones, gallstones, or other solid masses into smaller fragments that can be passed out of the body more easily. There are different methods of lithotripsy, but one of the most common techniques involves the use of shock waves to fragment the stones. This procedure is often employed when the stones are too large to be passed naturally or when they cause severe pain or blockages.

Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy (ESWL) is a common form of lithotripsy. In ESWL, shock waves are generated outside the body and directed at the location of the stone. These shock waves travel through the body and break the stone into smaller pieces that can be excreted through the urine.

Another method of lithotripsy involves the use of lasers to break down the stones. The choice of lithotripsy method depends on factors such as the size and location of the stones and the patient's overall health.

Lithotripsy is a less invasive alternative to traditional surgery for the removal of kidney stones and is associated with a shorter recovery time. However, it may not be suitable for all types of stones, and the choice of treatment should be made in consultation with a healthcare provider.
